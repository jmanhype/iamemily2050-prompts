RESEARCH TASK:

Conduct a full, multidisciplinary scholarly research report answering the 
question: **"What is a woman?"**

This question must be addressed across all of the following dimensions:

BIOLOGICAL: What does biology — including genetics, reproductive anatomy, 
chromosomal science, gamete theory, hormonal biology, and developmental 
biology — say defines female sex in humans? What is the standard scientific 
definition, and where does it have edge cases?

PHILOSOPHICAL: What are the major philosophical frameworks for defining 
"woman" — including biological essentialism, social constructionism, gender 
nominalism, cluster-kind theory, Sally Haslanger's revisionary account, 
Judith Butler's gender performativity, and any other significant positions? 
What are the strongest arguments for and against each?

ANTHROPOLOGICAL: How have different human cultures across history and 
geography defined and understood womanhood? What does cross-cultural 
variation tell us about the universality or constructedness of the category?

LEGAL: How do major legal systems currently define "woman" — including the 
UK Equality Act (post-2025 Supreme Court ruling), US federal definitions, 
EU frameworks, and international human rights law? Where do these definitions 
converge and diverge?

NEUROLOGICAL & PSYCHOLOGICAL: What does neuroscience say about sex 
differences in the brain? What is the peer-reviewed evidence on gender 
identity, gender dysphoria, and the relationship between brain structure and 
experienced gender?

HISTORICAL: How has the concept of "woman" evolved across Western and 
non-Western history? What role have medicine, law, religion, and politics 
played in shaping the definition?

CONTESTED TERRAIN: Clearly delineate (a) what is firmly established by 
scientific evidence, (b) what is genuinely debated among serious scholars, 
and (c) what is primarily a political or ideological position dressed as 
science.
