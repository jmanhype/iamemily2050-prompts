#CapCutseedance2

This is the prompt for CapCut agent and the storyboard.

Do not apply a cinematic filter yet.
Do not apply a LUT yet.
Do not add new color style yet.

First do only technical color matching.

Use Clip 1 as the fixed hero reference.
Do not change Clip 1 except very minor exposure correction.

Match Clip 2, Clip 3, and Clip 4 to Clip 1.

Correction order:
1. Exposure
2. Brightness / midtones
3. Shadow density
4. White balance
5. Skin tone
6. Saturation
7. Candlelight warmth
8. Blue-gray window tone

Target look before grading:
All four clips must look like they were shot in the same candlelit room, on the same camera, at the same time.

Clip-specific corrections:
Clip 2: raise midtones slightly, lift crushed shadows slightly, warm slightly, reduce saturation slightly.
Clip 3: raise midtones slightly, cool slightly, reduce red/orange warmth, reduce saturation slightly.
Clip 4: cool the image noticeably, reduce orange/yellow saturation, reduce highlight warmth, keep Eleanor’s face readable.

Do not add contrast.
Do not add a filter.
Do not add a LUT.
Do not make the image brighter overall.
Do not make skin orange.
Do not crush blacks.
Do not make the window cyan.

Stop after color matching only.

Now apply one subtle global Gothic period-drama look to the full 60-second reel.

Use low intensity only.
Warm amber candle highlights.
Cool blue-gray rainy window shadows.
Soft contrast.
Slightly desaturated color.
Natural pale skin.
Deep but readable shadows.

Do not change the individual clip matching.
Do not make Clip 4 orange.
Do not make Clip 2 blue.
Do not make Clip 3 red.
Do not use a modern social-media filter.
