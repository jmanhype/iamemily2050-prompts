Seedance V2 
You can use this system prompt in different ways and styles.

I highly recommend doing the storyboard first and give it to Seedance V2, I still have two unverisal prompts.

Concept:
A 15 second fast-cut anime montage about [CHARACTER_NAME], a [CHARACTER_TYPE], experiencing [EMOTIONAL_STATE] through [GESTURE_WORLD / OBJECT_WORLD / MEMORY_WORLD / CRAFT_WORLD].

Core emotional engine:
[One sentence describing what the character wants, fears, avoids, or cannot say.]

Reference assets:
Use Image 1 as the main character reference.
Use Image 2 as the environment reference.
Use Image 3 as [ABSENT_PERSON / REMEMBERED_PERSON / OBJECT / CRAFT / CREATURE] reference.
Optional: Use Video 1 as the motion/camera reference.

Format:
Japanese watercolor-style full-color anime, high cel count, tactile close-ups, cinematic macro detail, soft handheld camera, shallow depth of field.

Shot ratio:
21 character-moment shots + 7 b-roll shots.
B-roll at shots 03, 07, 11, 15, 19, 23, 27.

Camera:
Mostly macro and extreme macro. Avoid wide shots except emotional punctuation. Use silhouettes sparingly. Every shot must contain one clear motion.

Audio:
No dialogue. Use subtle sound design: [AUDIO_TEXTURES].
Minimal ambient score matching [EMOTIONAL_STATE].

B-roll rule:
B-roll does not explain the story. It externalizes the inner state through objects, light, weather, time, texture, or absence.

Sequence map:

01. Character hesitates before touching an important object.
02. Extreme macro of a repeated private gesture.
03. B-roll: emotional symbol #1.
04. Hand pressure, grip, release, or adjustment.
05. Object handled with tenderness or precision.
06. Face or breath against glass, metal, fabric, or light.
07. B-roll: weather, time, material, or sensory displacement.
08. Opening, closing, reaching, or withdrawing.
09. Extreme macro of a trace, stain, mark, line, scar, or texture.
10. Body reaction: shoulder, throat, jaw, breath, pulse.
11. B-roll: absence, material, time, or distorted presence.
12. Palm smooths, presses, aligns, folds, or repairs.
13. Screen, note, book, tool, photograph, or memory object.
14. Tiny facial tension or swallowed emotion.
15. B-roll: signal, pulse, lamp, clock, loop, echo.
16. Removing, fastening, unclasping, threading, tightening, or placing.
17. Mirror, silhouette, reflection, or self-avoidance.
18. Footstep, floorboard, chair shift, turn, pause, or recoil.
19. B-roll: unanswered signal, falling material, fading trace.
20. Extreme macro of touch following an old outline.
21. Wider silhouette with empty space or absorbed posture.
22. Object lifted toward light or inspected closely.
23. B-roll: time jump, physical echo, curtain, cushion, shadow.
24. Tiny discovery: hair, thread, petal, ash, eyelash, dust.
25. Breath, fog, smoke, steam, or fading mark.
26. Eye detail: blink, flutter, reflection, afterimage.
27. B-roll: final symbolic loop.
28. Wide or back-view ending: character stops, releases, leans back, or disappears.
